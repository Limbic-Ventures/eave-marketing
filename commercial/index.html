Eave Commercial

The building's memory — for the people who run buildings.

Eave brings the same capture-to-intelligence layer homeowners love to the organizations that steward property: businesses, HOAs and condo boards, churches and nonprofits, and small multifamily operators. Commercial from door one — not property-management software, not a CMMS.

How it works

One graph, however you steward property

Eave's data model is the building — rooms, systems, contractors, projects, and history, all tied to the structure itself. That means the same capture-to-intelligence layer that serves a single-family home also serves a storefront, an HOA's clubhouse, a church, or a 20-unit building. Nothing about the underlying model changes; only the roles, reports, and reasoning layered on top do.

Seats are always free — Eave Commercial is priced by the door or the location, never per user. Invite your whole board, staff, or maintenance team without a per-seat bill.

For leases, compliance, and tax questions, Eave documents and assembles the record — it never gives legal, tax, or insurance advice.

Editions

Five editions, one core

Mission

For: Tax-exempt organizations — churches, service nonprofits, community centers.

Signature: A stewardship report and a 990 occupancy-allocation report, plus donated-work and volunteer-hours capture.

Associations

For: Self-managed HOA and condo boards.

Signature: A living reserve study that updates as your buildings age, with board roles and term handoff.

Business

For: A single location — shop, café, clinic, or office.

Signature: A lease responsibility matrix that documents who-fixes-what from your actual lease, plus a compliance calendar.

Business Multi-Site

For: Small chains and franchisees, 2–20 locations.

Signature: Push one maintenance standard to every site, and compare vendor rates across locations.

Apartments

For: Owner-operators running 10–50 doors.

Signature: A unit-turn pipeline with sequenced checklists, and a multifamily compliance pack with evidence on file.

Editions roll out one at a time, starting with Mission. Pricing is published as each edition launches.
