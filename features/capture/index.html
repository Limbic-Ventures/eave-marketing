Features · Capture

Notice it once. Eave turns it into a plan.

You spot the running toilet, the cracked caulk, the loose deck board — and then you forget it until it's worse. Capture is the fix: say it, snap it, or type it, and Eave turns the thing you noticed into a scoped, prioritized project.

Three ways in

Speak, snap, or type

Speak

"The master bathroom toilet keeps running — probably a flapper, maybe $150."

Eave transcribes it and creates a project in the Master Bath, category Plumbing, with an estimated cost of $150–200.

Snap

A photo of the tub surround.

Vision AI diagnoses what it sees — cracked caulk detected — and drafts the project at medium priority from the image alone.

Type

"Deck boards feel loose near the steps."

Free-form text is parsed into a project flagged High priority for safety, so the risk to someone on the stairs doesn't sit at the bottom of a list.

The parsed result

From a sentence to a structured project

However you capture it, Eave returns the same thing: a clean project card with the fields already filled in. You didn't pick a room from a dropdown or guess at a category — the AI read your description and did it.

Every result card carries a title, room, category, priority, estimated cost, and a permit flag — so you know at a glance whether it's a five-minute fix or something that needs a licensed contractor and a pulled permit before anyone starts.

Nothing saves without your say. You review the card, correct anything that's off, and add it to your queue when it's right.

Questions

About Capture

How does AI capture work?

You describe what you noticed in plain language — spoken, photographed, or typed — and Eave's AI structures it into a project. It fills in the title, room, category, priority, estimated cost, and a permit flag, then shows you the result to review before anything saves. Nothing is added without your say.

Can I capture by voice?

Yes. Tap the microphone and say it the way you'd tell a friend — for example, "the master bathroom toilet keeps running, probably a flapper, maybe $150." Eave transcribes it and creates a project in the Master Bath under Plumbing with an estimated cost of $150–200, ready for you to confirm.

What does Eave figure out from a photo?

Snap a photo and vision AI diagnoses what it sees — for example, detecting cracked caulk and setting a medium priority. It drafts the project fields from the image so you're not typing out what the camera can already tell. As always, you review the result before it's saved.
