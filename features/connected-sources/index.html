Product · Connected Sources

Your inbox already has your home's history. Eave finds it.

The receipts, warranties, and contractor confirmations that make up your home's record are already scattered across your email and calendar. Connect Gmail and Eave surfaces the home-related pieces for you — read-only, and nothing saves until you review it.

What connects today

Gmail and calendar, live now

Today you can connect Gmail and your calendar. That's usually all it takes to start filling in your home's history, because the important records tend to already be there — an appliance receipt, a warranty registration, a confirmation from the plumber you booked last spring.

Once connected, Eave watches for the home-related items among them and pulls them out: a receipt gets matched to the right appliance, a warranty date gets logged, a contractor confirmation becomes a contact in your directory. The work of hunting through old email disappears.

Store accounts — Home Depot, Lowe's, Amazon — and smart-home connections are rolling out. They're not live yet, so we won't pretend otherwise; when they land, they'll extend the same idea to your purchase and device history.

Connected sources on the desktop app: Gmail and calendar link today, with store and smart-home connections rolling out.

What Eave reads

Read-only, and only your home

Access is read-only. Eave never sends email on your behalf and never changes anything in your inbox or calendar. It looks, it surfaces, and that's the extent of it.

It also looks for one thing: your home. Eave surfaces home-related receipts, warranty registrations, and contractor or service confirmations — not personal correspondence, not everything you've ever received. The goal is a fuller home record, not a full read of your mail.

If you want the specifics on how that access works and how your data is handled, our data and privacy page covers it in plain terms.

On mobile, Eave surfaces the home items it found — each one waiting for you to review before it's saved.

You review first

Nothing saves without your say

Eave surfaces, you decide

Connecting a source doesn't hand Eave the keys to your records. It surfaces what it finds — a receipt, a warranty expiry, a contractor's confirmation — and holds it for your review. You choose what to keep, correct anything that's off, and decide where it belongs. Nothing lands in your home knowledge base until you say so.

Why it works this way

Your home's record is only useful if it's accurate, and the fastest way to erode trust in it is to let a system save things you never checked. Review-before-save keeps you in control and keeps the record clean — the same principle applies whether you capture something by voice, by photo, or from a connected inbox.

In practice
How do I keep track of home warranties?

The dishwasher receipt lands in your inbox and Eave notices it — the purchase date, the model, the warranty term. You confirm it once in the review queue, and that's the last time you think about it until it matters: a reminder before the warranty lapses, while a repair claim is still free.

No spreadsheet, no shoebox of receipts, no trying to remember which drawer the paperwork went in. The warranty tracks itself because the record filled in from the source.

Questions

About connected sources

What can I connect to Eave today?

Today you can connect Gmail and your calendar. From there, Eave surfaces the home-related receipts, warranties, and contractor confirmations already sitting in your inbox and schedule. Store accounts like Home Depot, Lowe's, and Amazon, along with smart-home connections, are rolling out and not live yet.

What does Eave read from my email?

Eave connects to Gmail with read-only access and looks only for home-related items — purchase receipts, warranty registrations, and contractor or service confirmations. It surfaces those for you rather than combing through personal mail. It never sends email on your behalf and never changes anything in your inbox.

Does anything save automatically?

No. Nothing saves to your home records without your review first. Eave surfaces what it finds — a receipt, a warranty expiry, a contractor — and you decide what to keep and where it goes. Review-before-save is a core part of how Eave works: nothing saves without your say.
