Your home's memory

More active than insurance — protection for the home you already have.

Homeowners insurance pays you after something breaks, and only if the paperwork holds up. Eave Guardian works before: an evidence vault ready for every claim, a 10-year forecast for what's coming, and the documentation insurers actually ask for — so nothing is scrambled together the day you need it.

Evidence vault

One tap assembles the claim packet — before you need it

Every photo, receipt, contractor invoice, and warranty document you've added to Eave lives in the evidence vault — dated, organized, and tied to the room or system it belongs to. It's the record insurers and warranty providers ask for when something goes wrong, built up automatically instead of dug up in a panic.

When you need to file, one tap assembles a claim packet: the relevant photos, receipts, and maintenance history pulled together into a single document. Guardian documents and assembles your evidence; your insurer and your policy decide coverage.

The evidence vault keeps every receipt, photo, and warranty tied to the system it belongs to.

Capex forecast

Know what's coming before it's an emergency

Every major system in your home has a lifespan — a roof, a water heater, an HVAC unit. Guardian's 10-year capex forecast reads the age and condition of your systems and lays out roughly when each one is likely to need replacement, so the big expenses show up on a timeline instead of a doorstep.

That's the difference between budgeting for a new water heater two years out and finding out the morning of a cold shower. The forecast turns "someday" into a plan you can actually save for.

The forecast reads your home's systems and lays out what's likely to need replacing, and roughly when.

Carrier-discount checklist

The documentation insurers actually ask for

Many insurers offer discounts for homes with documented upgrades — new roofs, updated electrical, monitored systems — but only if you can prove it. Guardian's carrier-discount checklist lays out what that proof typically looks like, so the paperwork is ready when you ask your carrier what you qualify for.

It's a checklist, not a quote or a guarantee — what you qualify for is between you and your carrier. Guardian's job is making sure the documentation isn't the reason you miss out.

Documented upgrades, ready to hand your carrier when you ask about a discount.

Annual virtual audit review

A yearly checkpoint so your snapshot never goes stale

A standing checkpoint, not a one-time capture

Once a year, Guardian reviews your home's records against what's changed — new systems, completed projects, updated warranties — and flags what needs a refresh. It's the difference between a snapshot taken once and a record that stays current as your home does.

Built for the moments that matter

An up-to-date record is worth the most exactly when you need it most: filing a claim, selling the home, or shopping for a new policy. The annual review keeps the evidence vault ready for those moments instead of years out of date.

Telemetry fusion Rolling out

Sensor data, fused into the same record

Leak, electrical, solar, and thermostat sensors

Guardian is rolling out telemetry fusion — pulling in data from leak sensors, electrical monitors, solar systems, and smart thermostats and folding it into the same home record as your photos and receipts. Instead of a separate app per device, one picture of what your home's systems are actually doing.

What "rolling out" means here

Telemetry fusion is shipping in phases rather than available to every home on day one. Guardian subscribers get it as it ships, at no added cost, alongside the rest of Guardian at launch: the evidence vault, claim packets, the capex forecast, the carrier-discount checklist, the annual virtual audit, and priority support.

Questions

About Eave Guardian

What's in the evidence vault?

The evidence vault holds the records that prove your home's condition and upkeep over time: dated photos, receipts, contractor invoices, warranty documents, and the maintenance history behind them. It's the material an insurer or warranty provider actually asks for when something goes wrong, already organized and ready before you need it.

How do claim packets work?

One tap assembles a claim packet from your evidence vault — the photos, receipts, and maintenance records relevant to the claim, organized into a single document. Guardian documents and assembles your evidence; your insurer and your policy decide coverage. Guardian doesn't file the claim or negotiate on your behalf, and it isn't a guarantee of payout — it's the paperwork done ahead of time.

What does "rolling out" mean for telemetry?

Telemetry fusion — pulling in leak, electrical, solar, and thermostat sensor data — is an active part of the Guardian roadmap and is rolling out in phases rather than available to every home on day one. Guardian subscribers get it as it ships, at no added cost, alongside the rest of Guardian at launch: the evidence vault, claim packets, the capex forecast, the carrier-discount checklist, the annual virtual audit, and priority support.

Guardian vs Intelligence — what's the difference?

Eave Intelligence is $2.99/mo or $24/yr and stays available — it's the planning layer, telling you what to do next with a seasonal prep digest and a ranked weekly action queue. Eave Guardian is $24.99/mo or $199/yr and adds the protection layer on top: an evidence vault with one-tap claim packets, a 10-year capex forecast, a carrier-discount checklist, an annual virtual audit review, telemetry fusion (rolling out), and priority support.
