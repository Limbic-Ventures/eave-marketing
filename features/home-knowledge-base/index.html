Features · Home Knowledge Base

Everything about your home, in one place you'll actually find later.

Most people can't answer basic questions about the home they live in — the furnace filter size, when the water heater went in, who fixed the roof, what paint is in the hallway. The home knowledge base is where those answers live: a complete, searchable record of your rooms, appliances, contractors, and repairs.

Room Inventory

Snap a room, get a record

Take one photo of a room and Eave fills in the floors, walls, finishes, and fixtures — each with an AI confidence level you can check. It's the fastest way to turn a house full of details into a record you can search, and every field stays editable before you save.

Later, when a contractor asks what the hallway paint is or how the bathroom floor is finished, the answer is already in your pocket.

Appliance Registry

The specs, found for you

Add an appliance and Eave researches the model — pulling in the details you'd otherwise dig through a manual to find. Model number, purchase date, warranty expiry, service intervals, and a replacement forecast, so you're never surprised by an aging system.

In practice
What size furnace filter do I need?

You snap the data plate on the furnace once. Eave reads it — a Carrier 59TP6, installed 2015 — and files the filter size for good: 16×25×1, MERV 11. The next time a filter change comes due, you don't measure anything or dig through a drawer for the old one. The size is already on the appliance record, the reminder already knows it, and the replacement forecast already says the unit itself is good until around 2038.

The question "what size furnace filter do I need" stops being a question. That's the whole point of a knowledge base — you answer it once, and your home remembers.

Contractor Directory

Your trusted network, not a lead-gen list

Photograph a business card, keep the contact

Snap a business card and Eave saves the contact — name, trade, and how to reach them. When something breaks, "I have no idea who to call" stops being true: your plumber, your electrician, and the person who did the roof are already here, tied to the work they did. This is your network, kept for you — not a marketplace pushing new vendors at you.

Connected Sources

Records that fill in on their own

Gmail and calendar today

Connect Gmail and your calendar and Eave surfaces the home-related receipts, warranties, and contractor emails already sitting in your inbox — so your knowledge base grows without you typing it all in. Access is read-only, and nothing is saved until you review it.

Store accounts and smart home — rolling out

Connections to store accounts and smart-home devices are on the way, not live yet. When they arrive, purchases and device details will flow in the same way — found for you, reviewed by you.

Receipt Scanner

A receipt becomes a record

Photograph a receipt, link the details

Take a photo of a receipt and Eave reads the line items and prices, then links them to the right appliance or project. The filter you bought lands on the furnace; the part for the dishwasher lands on the dishwasher. Warranty math and replacement history stop being guesswork — the paper trail is already in the record.

FAQ

Common questions

What is a home knowledge base?

A home knowledge base is a single, searchable record of everything about your home — every room, appliance, contractor, and repair, with the details attached. In Eave it holds finishes and fixtures room by room, appliance models with their warranty and service history, your trusted contractors, and the receipts that tie it all together. It is the memory of your home, so the questions you can never answer later have answers.

Does Eave research appliance specs automatically?

Yes. When you add an appliance, Eave researches the model and fills in the details you would otherwise dig through a manual to find — things like filter size, service intervals, and warranty term. For a Carrier furnace, for example, that can mean a 16×25×1 filter, a warranty running through 2029, and a rough replacement forecast around 2038. Everything it suggests is editable before you save.

Is my home data private?

Your home data is yours. Nothing saves without your say — Eave shows you what it found and you review it before it is stored. For the full picture of how your data is handled, see our data privacy page.
