Features · Intelligence

Eave tells you what to do next, and why it matters now.

Most home maintenance apps hand you a checklist and leave the thinking to you. Eave Intelligence reads your home's records, its age, and the season, then turns all of it into a plan — a seasonal prep digest and a ranked weekly action queue that explain what matters, and why now.

Seasonal prep digest

A seasonal home maintenance plan, before the season turns

Ahead of each season, Eave sends a prep digest — "Winter is 14 days away. Here's what your home needs." It isn't a generic list. It reads your home's age and systems and the records you've built up, then lays out the work worth doing before the weather changes.

Each item comes with the reasoning: what the task is, why it matters this time of year, roughly how many hours it takes, and whether it's a reasonable DIY job or one worth hiring out. So you're not just told to clean the gutters — you know why now, how long it runs, and whether to pick up a ladder or pick up the phone.

The result is the difference between a reminder and a plan. You open the digest already knowing what your weekend could look like and what it's worth handing off.

The seasonal view lays out the tasks your home needs before the weather turns, each with its own reasoning.

In practice
When do I flush the water heater — and when should I replace it?

Fall arrives and the digest is already written: flush the water heater, check the exterior before the first freeze, clear the gutters. Each task says why it matters for a home your age and how long it takes — a real seasonal maintenance checklist, built from your property, not a generic list off the internet.

The water heater installed in 2015 carries a replacement forecast, so you're budgeting for it two years out instead of finding out the morning of a cold shower. Knowing when to replace it stops being guesswork.

This week's action queue

Up to five things that need you now — ranked and reasoned

The weekly action queue is the short list. Rather than showing everything at once, Eave surfaces up to five items that genuinely need attention now, ordered by what matters most. Clear those, and the next set moves up.

It draws from what's actually happening in your home: maintenance that's gone overdue, high-priority projects that have stalled, and warranties that are about to expire. Each item carries the reason it made the cut — so you're never guessing why it's there or whether it can wait.

It's the part of Eave that answers the question most homeowners can't: of everything my home needs, what should I actually do this week? A ranked, reasoned five is far easier to act on than a backlog that never ends.

The home dashboard leads with what's active and due, so the week's priorities are the first thing you see.

Tied to your score

Every action moves the Upkeep Score

The score sets the priorities

Your Upkeep Score is a live 0–100 read of your home's health — open projects, overdue tasks, and the age and systems of the home, all in one number. Intelligence uses that same picture to decide what belongs in the digest and the action queue. The things dragging your score down are the things it puts in front of you first.

Acting on the queue raises it

Because the score is live, it moves as you work. Clear an overdue task or close out a stalled project from the action queue and the number ticks up; let a seasonal task slip past due and it drifts down. Intelligence closes the loop — it tells you what to do next, and doing it is exactly what improves the score you're watching.

The Upkeep Score is free; the plan is the upgrade

The Upkeep Score itself is part of Eave's free tier. Intelligence is the paid upgrade that turns that number into a plan — the seasonal digest, the ranked queue, a twelve-month upkeep plan, and materials estimates. You can watch your score for free and add Intelligence when you want it to tell you what to do about it.

The upgrade

Free to start, Intelligence when you're ready

What stays free

Eave is free for one property, forever, and runs in any browser at app.eavehome.app. That includes your home knowledge base — rooms, appliances, contractors, and repairs — voice, photo, and text capture, and the live Upkeep Score. You never have to pay to keep your home's records or watch its health.

What Intelligence adds

Eave Intelligence is from $2.99/mo, $24/yr, or $49 for a lifetime license. It adds the AI project queue, a twelve-month upkeep plan, the seasonal prep digest, ranked priorities, and materials estimates — the parts of Eave that don't just remember your home, but tell you what to do about it next.
