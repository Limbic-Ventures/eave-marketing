Features · Upkeep Score

A single number for how your home is actually doing.

Most people can't answer a simple question: is my home in good shape right now, or is it slipping? The Upkeep Score answers it — a live 0–100 reading that reflects what your home actually needs, not a checklist someone else wrote.

The number

A live score, not a static checklist

The Upkeep Score is a single 0–100 figure that sits at the top of your home dashboard. It reads three things at once: the projects you have open, the maintenance tasks that are overdue, and the age and systems of your home. Put together, they describe the health of the home as it stands today.

Because it's live, the number moves as your home moves. Close out a project and it ticks up. Let a high-priority repair stall or a seasonal task slip past due, and it drifts down. Nothing about it is generic — two identical houses with different backlogs will show different scores.

A to-do list tells you there's work. A number tells you whether you're winning or losing — and that's the difference between something you glance at and something you act on.

The home dashboard leads with the score, then the projects and tasks driving it.

What moves it

What raises the score, and what lowers it

Raises it

Closing out open projects, staying ahead of maintenance so nothing goes overdue, and keeping your home's records complete. The score rewards the work that genuinely keeps a home healthy — clearing the backlog, not adding to a list.

Lowers it

Letting a high-priority project stall, missing a seasonal task, or an appliance aging past its expected service life with nothing logged. The older or more system-heavy the home, the more upkeep it needs to hold a strong score — which is exactly the honesty a generic checklist can't give you.

Why a number beats a to-do list

A list of tasks never tells you how you're doing overall — you can clear ten items and still be behind, or ignore the list entirely and not feel it until something breaks. A single score you can watch trend up or down turns upkeep from a pile of chores into something you can actually manage. That's the point: a number that means something is easier to act on than a list that never ends.

Questions

About the Upkeep Score

How is the Upkeep Score calculated?

The Upkeep Score is a live 0–100 reading of your home's health. It reflects what's actually happening: how many projects are open, how many maintenance tasks are overdue, and the age and systems of your home. It updates as your home changes, so the number always reflects reality rather than a checklist someone else wrote.

What raises my score?

Closing out open projects, staying current on maintenance so tasks aren't overdue, and keeping your home's records complete all move the score up. Letting high-priority projects stall or maintenance slip past due moves it down. The score rewards the work that genuinely keeps a home healthy, not busywork.

Yes. The Upkeep Score is part of Eave's free tier for one property, forever, and runs in any browser at app.eavehome.app. The deeper Intelligence features — the ranked action queue, twelve-month upkeep plan, and seasonal prep digest — are part of Eave Intelligence, from $2.99/mo.
