For home buyers

Buying a home?
Know what you're actually taking on.

Before you fall for the kitchen, understand the roof, the furnace, and everything the inspection is trying to tell you — and move in with a plan instead of a shoebox of PDFs.

The report you skimmed once

The inspection told you. Then you forgot.

"We got the inspection report, skimmed it, closed, and then spent two years rediscovering everything it already told us."

A home inspection is one of the most useful documents you'll ever be handed — and one of the most quickly filed away. In the rush of closing, it gets a skim and a signature, then disappears into a folder. Two years later you're paying for a water heater you were warned about on page nine.

It doesn't have to go that way. The report is a starting point, not a formality — and Eave is where it turns into something you'll actually use.

From report to real plan

How Eave helps you buy with your eyes open

  1. 1

    Turn the inspection into a plan

    Import the report; Eave sorts what's urgent, what's soon, and what's someday — with rough costs. The ranked plan replaces a PDF you'd never open twice.

  2. 2

    Buy with your eyes open

    Understand the real cost of ownership before you sign, so surprises don't become regrets. When you know the roof has five years left, you can plan for it instead of being blindsided by it.

  3. 3

    Move in already set up

    Your new home starts with its systems, ages, and to-dos in place — day one, not year two. The home knowledge base is already built, waiting for you to walk in.

The things buyers actually ask
