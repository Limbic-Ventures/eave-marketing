For home sellers

Selling your home?
Don't let the last month be a scramble.

The repairs, the paint touch-ups, the little things your agent and inspector point out — Eave keeps the whole list in one place, so you actually get to them before the photos.

The part that sneaks up on you

The list you meant to keep

"My agent walked through a year before we listed and rattled off a dozen easy wins. By the time we listed, I'd forgotten half of them."

Getting a home ready to sell isn't one big job — it's forty small ones, spread across a year, mentioned in passing and then forgotten. The caulk line in the guest bath. The garage door that needs repainting. The furnace that's due for service. None of them are hard on their own. What's hard is remembering all of them at once, in the weeks before you list, when everything else is happening too.

Eave is where that list lives so it doesn't live in your head — captured the moment it's mentioned, sorted into what actually moves the needle, and ready long before the sign goes in the yard.

From scattered notes to a real plan

How Eave helps you get ready

  1. 1

    Capture every recommendation

    Snap a photo or say it out loud the moment your agent or inspector mentions it. Nothing lives only in your memory — each note becomes a scoped project with a room, a category, and a rough cost.

  2. 2

    Get a plan, not a pile

    Eave sorts the list into what to do first — most value, least effort — for the time you actually have before listing. Eave Intelligence turns the backlog into a ranked queue instead of an overwhelming pile.

  3. 3

    Fix it before it's leverage

    Handle the small stuff early so it never becomes a repair request or a price chip at the negotiating table. A home that's clearly cared for gives the buyer less to push back on.

  4. 4

    Hand the buyer the whole story

    At closing, pass along your home's record — projects, materials, vendors — as proof it was cared for. Your home knowledge base can transfer with the sale, and a documented home negotiates better.

Start earlier, sell easier.

The six months before you list are worth more than the six weeks. Give yourself room to do the medium stuff — repaint the garage door, service the systems — on your schedule, not under the gun. The earlier you start the list, the more of it becomes done instead of disclosed.

Selling with an agent?

When you’re ready, hand them a clean summary of your home — everything you’ve documented, in one place. It’s your record, so you choose what to share and when.

The things sellers actually ask

Questions, answered.

When should I start?

The earlier the better; even a year out helps. If you're thinking about selling, it's time to start a list. The six months before you list are worth more than the six weeks — they're what turn a stressful scramble into a calm handoff.

Do I need my agent to use Eave?

No. You can start on your own today. Eave captures whatever your agent or inspector points out — you don't need them in the app for it to work for you.

Can I pass my home's record to the buyer?

Yes — your home's memory can transfer with the sale, a maintenance head start for the new owner. Projects, materials, and vendors go with the house instead of walking out the door with you.

Is it free?

Start free; add Eave Intelligence for $2.99 a month when you want the full plan — the ranked queue by value and effort, and the 12-month schedule.
