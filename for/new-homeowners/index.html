For new homeowners

You just bought a house. Here's the manual it didn't come with.

The keys came with a mortgage, not an instruction book. Eave turns your new address into a living record of the home — what it has, what it needs, and what to do first — so the first year feels less like guessing.

The part nobody warned you about

"Nobody tells you about this stuff when you buy a house"

"I don't even know what I don't know."

New homeowners tend to arrive at the same feeling within a few weeks: the excitement wears off and a quiet backlog takes its place. A vent that whistles. A water heater with no known age. A furnace filter of unknown size. There is no previous owner on speed dial and no clear picture of what the house actually needs.

You are not behind, and you are not doing it wrong. Most people simply never get a starting point. In fact, 79% of new homeowners say they'd be better prepared if they had a personalized home manual — the exact thing a closing folder never includes.

Your first month, made simple

How Eave helps you settle in

Set up from your address

Enter the address you just closed on and Eave pre-fills a profile for the home — property type, approximate age, and the systems a house like yours tends to have. Review the details, add a space and a room, and you have a foundation to build on in a couple of minutes.

Capture the backlog

Everything you've been mentally noting since move-in has somewhere to go. Say it, snap it, or type it — "the master bathroom toilet keeps running, probably a flapper" — and capture turns it into a scoped project with a room, a category, a priority, and a rough cost. The backlog stops living in your head.

Build the knowledge base

As you go, your home writes its own record. Rooms, appliances, contractors, and repairs collect into a searchable home knowledge base — the furnace model and filter size, the water heater's install date, who you called and what it cost. It's the manual the house never came with, and it's the same record that makes insuring, refinancing, or selling far less painful later.

Get the weekly queue

Once there's something to work with, Eave Intelligence turns the pile into a plan: a ranked weekly action queue of what needs attention now, a 12-month upkeep schedule tuned to your home, and a seasonal prep digest before each season lands. You stop wondering what's next — Eave tells you, and tells you why.

New homeowner questions

The things you're actually wondering

I just bought a home — where do I start?

Start with your address. Eave uses it to pre-fill a profile for your home, then walks you through adding your first space and room. From there, capture anything you already know needs attention — the running toilet, the loose deck board, the vague sense that the water heater is old. Each one becomes a structured project, and your home's record starts building itself.

Do I need to know anything technical?

No. That's the point. You describe things in plain language — speak, snap a photo, or type — and Eave figures out the room, category, likely cause, priority, and a rough cost. You don't need to know the difference between a flapper and a fill valve; you just need to notice the toilet keeps running.

Is it free?

Yes. Eave is free for one property, forever, and runs in any browser at app.eavehome.app. Eave Intelligence — the ranked weekly queue, 12-month upkeep plan, and seasonal prep digest — is an optional upgrade from $2.99 a month. Native iOS and Android apps are coming soon.
