Capturing your home: voice, photo, or text

Add anything to Eave by speaking, snapping a photo, or typing — every capture is a draft you review before saving.

Eave is your home's memory, and capture is how you add to it. Describe something out loud, snap a photo, or type a few words, and Eave turns it into a project you can review and save.

The three ways to capture

Speak it (voice) Tap the mic and describe the work in plain language, like "the gutters need cleaning before winter." Your words are transcribed right in your browser and dropped into the text box for you to review. Voice works in Chrome, Edge, and Safari; if your browser doesn't support it, the mic button simply won't appear. Voice is currently English only.

*Best for:* capturing a thought quickly, hands-free, while you're walking the house.

Snap a photo Choose "Snap a photo" to open your camera or pick an image. Eave looks at the picture and drafts a project from what it sees, such as a stained ceiling or a worn deck board.

*Best for:* things easier to show than describe, like visible damage or wear.

Describe it (text) Type a description of the project and let Eave parse it. On a keyboard you can press Cmd+Return (or Ctrl+Return) to parse without reaching for the mouse.

*Best for:* details you already know, or when you're somewhere quiet.

What happens after you capture

All three methods create the same thing: a draft you can review. Eave fills in a title, description, room, category, status, priority, estimated cost, contractor, and notes based on what you gave it.

Nothing is saved until you say so. Read the draft, fix anything that's off, then choose Save to Projects. After you save, Eave quietly enriches the project in the background to add helpful detail, but your project is already safely stored either way.

Tips for good photos

  • Get close, but keep the whole issue in frame. One clear subject beats a wide, busy shot.
  • Use good light. Natural daylight or a bright room helps Eave see materials and damage accurately.
  • Hold steady. A sharp, in-focus photo reads far better than a blurry one.
  • For a spec label or model plate, photograph it straight-on so the text is legible. (To add an appliance from its label, use the assistant in Systems & Appliances.)
  • Big documents? Photograph the key pages rather than uploading a large PDF. Eave automatically shrinks photos before upload, so images are usually the easier path.

Common questions

Quick answers

Do I need to be online for voice to work?

Voice transcription happens in your browser. Turning your words into a project draft, and photo and text capture, use Eave's AI, so you'll want a connection for those.

Can I edit what the AI got wrong?

Yes. Every capture is a draft first. Adjust any field before saving, and nothing is stored until you choose Save to Projects.

Is capture a paid feature?

No. Voice, photo, and text capture are included for free.

Why doesn't my mic button show up?

Voice capture needs a browser that supports speech recognition (Chrome, Edge, or Safari). Unsupported browsers hide the button. Everything else still works through photo and text.
