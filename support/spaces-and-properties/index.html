Spaces and properties

How your property and its spaces fit together, why Main House can't be deleted, and what multi-property adds.

Eave organizes your home in two layers: your property (the address itself) and the spaces inside it (the distinct structures and zones you keep up). Here's how the two fit together and what to expect as your home grows.

Properties vs. spaces

A property is a single address — your home. Today, every account has one property, and it's always the center of everything Eave tracks: your tasks, projects, appliances, and history all live under it.

A space is a distinct part of that property. Your Main House is one space; a detached garage, a shed, an ADU, a pool house, or the yard can each be their own space too. Breaking your home into spaces lets you keep upkeep, inventory, and projects organized by where they actually happen, instead of one long undifferentiated list.

You'll find everything under Spaces & areas in the sidebar, in the Property group.

Why Main House can't be deleted

Every property starts with a Main House, and it's there to stay. It's the anchor that the rest of your home hangs off of, so Eave keeps it in place — you can't delete it. You can rename it and add details, but it will always be there as home base.

Every other space is fully yours to manage. Add the ones that match your property, edit them, and remove any you no longer need. There's no wrong number of spaces — start with just the Main House, or map out every structure on the lot.

Adding and organizing spaces

  1. Open Spaces & areas from the sidebar.
  2. Add a space for each structure or zone you want to track separately (garage, shed, yard, ADU, and so on).
  3. Give it a clear name and any details you'd like to remember.

When you create a project or task, Eave ties it to the right space. If your home has only the Main House, that happens quietly in the background — you don't have to think about it.

Multiple properties are coming

Right now every account covers one property, which is all most homeowners need. Multi-property support is coming soon as a separate add-on, letting you manage up to five properties under one account.

The property switcher in the top-left corner is always there — tapping Add property shows you the multi-property option as it becomes available.

Common questions

Quick answers

Can I rename the Main House?

Yes. You can rename it and add details — you just can't delete it.

How many spaces can I have?

As many as you need. Add a space for every structure or zone on your property.

How many properties can I add?

One today, on any plan. Multi-property (up to five) is coming soon as a separate add-on.

Do I have to assign every project to a space?

No. If you only have the Main House, Eave assigns it for you automatically.

Where do I manage all this?

Under Spaces & areas in the sidebar; the property switcher lives in the top-left corner.
