Support · Using Eave

Using Eave day to day

AI Capture, room scanning, connected sources, shopping lists, seasonal maintenance, and how projects move from "noticed" to "done."

FAQs

Common questions

How does AI Capture work?

Speak or type a project description in the Capture tab. Eave's AI extracts the title, room, category, priority, estimated cost, and whether a permit is likely required — then adds it to your project queue for review.

How does photo scanning work?

In the Memory tab, open any room and tap Scan Room. The photo uploads to secure storage, then AI analyzes it and auto-fills floor material, wall color, ceiling height, and flags visible issues. You can edit anything it gets wrong.

What's the difference between a Space and a Room?

A Space is a distinct structure on your property — Main House, Detached Garage, ADU. Rooms live inside Spaces. The Memory Builder documents every room within each space.

How do Connected Sources work?

Once you connect a source (like Gmail or a retailer), Eave scans for home-related emails and purchases. Nothing saves automatically — you review and confirm each imported item first. See Data & Privacy for exactly what Eave does and doesn't read.

Can I add more than one property?

Multi-property is coming soon as an add-on to Eave Intelligence — each additional property (2–5) gets its own full Eave at +$39/yr, and 6+ properties is Eave Commercial. Get notified when it launches.

In Depth

AI features

How AI Capture turns notes into projects

Type or speak a description in the Capture tab. Eave's AI parses it into structured fields — title, room, category, priority, estimated cost, and permit flag — then queues it for your review.

Scanning rooms with photo analysis

Open a room in Memory and tap "Scan Room." Your photo is uploaded and analyzed. AI fills in flooring, wall color, ceiling height, and flags visible issues. Everything is editable.

Reviewing and correcting AI suggestions

AI suggestions appear in a review queue before saving. Tap any field to edit. Corrections help Eave improve over time.

Generating shopping lists from projects

On any project, tap "Generate List." Eave produces a materials and tools list based on the project description. You can edit, copy, or export it.

In Depth

Connected sources

Connecting Gmail for receipt and warranty detection

Go to Connected Sources and tap "Connect Gmail." Authorize read-only access. Eave scans for home-related emails — receipts, warranties, contractor confirmations.

Importing past purchases

After connecting a source, detected items appear in your review queue. Tap "Add to Eave" on any item to save it. Nothing imports without your confirmation.

In Depth

Maintenance & projects

Seasonal maintenance tracker

The Maintenance tab shows a checklist of seasonal tasks based on your property type and location. Check off tasks as you complete them — Eave logs the date automatically.

Project statuses, priorities, and filters

Projects can be New, In Progress, or Done. Priority levels are Low, Medium, High, and Urgent. Use filters in the Intelligence tab to sort and surface what needs attention.

Resolution paths: DIY, Learn, or Hire

Each project has a resolution path. DIY means you're handling it. Learn shows relevant guides. Hire surfaces contractors from your directory or the Eave network.

The contractor directory

Add contractors you've used under Contacts. Include specialty, notes, and rating. When a project needs a hire, Eave surfaces relevant contacts from your directory first.
